3)How do you get rid of Formula that omits adjacent cells?
If you have a formula in Excel that omits adjacent cells that you want to include,you can modify the formula to include those cells by adjusting the 
range of cells that the formula references.

Click on the cell containing the formula that you want to modify.
Click on the formula bar at the top of the Excel window to edit the formula.
Adjust the range of cells in the formula to include the cells you want to include. For example, if your original formula was =SUM(A1:A8), and you want to include cell A9 in the sum, change the formula to =SUM(A1:A9).
Press "Enter" to confirm the new formula.

4)How do you select non-adjacent cells in Excel 2016?
Click on the first cell or range of cells that you want to select.
Hold down the "Ctrl" key on your keyboard.
Click on any additional cells or ranges of cells that you want to select. You can click and drag over a range of cells to select them, or click on individual cells to add them to the selection.
Release the "Ctrl" key when you have selected all the cells you need.

5)What happens if you choose a column, hold down the Alt key and press the letters ocw in quick succession?
If you choose a column in Excel, hold down the Alt key, and press the letters "ocw" in quick succession, it will hide the selected column.

6)If you right-click on a row reference number and click on Insert, where will the row be added?
If you right-click on a row reference number in Excel and click on "Insert," the new row will be inserted above the row that you right-clicked on.
